Curriculum

Getting Unstuck is a computer science curriculum for students in 4th, 5th, and 6th grade, using the Scratch programming environment. The curriculum aims to increase fluency with code through open-ended student-directed projects that are constrained in only one way: by using a particular Scratch block (or set of blocks).

Getting Unstuck is composed of 10 projects. Each project is organized around the creative computing design studio framework, which emphasizes learning through exploring, creating, sharing, and reflecting.

Getting Unstuck is intended for teachers and students who have previous experience with Scratch; for an introductory creative computing experience with Scratch, we recommend the Creative Computing Curriculum.

Getting Unstuck is available in Google Slides as the full curriculum or individual projects. Preview in Google Slides, download as a PDF, or edit by making a copy to remix and adapt the slides to your teaching context.

Getting started

The Getting Unstuck curriculum is organized around understanding the classroom as a creative computing design studio. Through the design studio framework, the Getting Unstuck curriculum supports students in making projects that deepen their creative and conceptual fluency.
