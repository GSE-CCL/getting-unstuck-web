10 Days of Getting Unstuck

In the Creative Computing Lab, we often look to create playful learning opportunities where learners can experience "hard fun," which Seymour Papert explained as "fun because it was hard rather than in spite of being hard." It can be hard and frustrating for learners to continue to have fun if they get stuck, but practice using strategies to get unstuck can support learners in continuing their work. The Getting Unstuck project was inspired by the idea that getting stuck and unstuck are natural and important aspects of the creative process.

From July 6–17, 2020, we sent out daily emails asking you to explore an idea, create a Scratch project, share that project to the daily studio, and reflect on their creative process. We designed this learning experience for PK–12 teachers who are passionate or curious about supporting creative work with Scratch (a graphical programming language and online community being used by millions of young people around the world) in the classroom. Even in such a short amount of time (only two weeks!), we saw everyone challenging themselves to explore new things, reflecting deeply on their process, and supporting and encouraging others in their work. Here we'll share some of the exciting things we saw.
